# Why Use Hermes?

## The Problem

Modern development workflows are fragmented. You switch between terminals, browsers, docs, editors, and dashboards dozens of times a day. Every context switch costs focus and time.

## The Solution: Hermes

Hermes is an AI agent that lives in your terminal and brings everything together in one place. It thinks, acts, and remembers — so you can focus on what matters.

## Key Reasons to Choose Hermes

### 1. One Interface for Everything

Instead of juggling tools, talk to Hermes. It can search the web, run commands, edit files, manage tasks, send messages, and more — all from a single conversation.

### 2. Persistent Memory

Hermes remembers who you are, what you prefer, and what you've worked on. No more repeating yourself across sessions. It learns your conventions and adapts.

### 3. Skill-Based Workflows

Hermes comes with a library of reusable skills — from GitHub PR workflows to ML model deployment to smart home control. Skills encode best practices so you don't have to remember every step.

### 4. Autonomous Execution

Tell Hermes what you want, not how to do it. It plans multi-step tasks, runs commands, handles errors, and reports back. You stay in the driver's seat without micromanaging.

### 5. Sub-Agent Delegation

Complex tasks can be split across parallel sub-agents. Hermes coordinates them, keeping your main context clean while work happens in the background.

### 6. Scheduling & Automation

Set up recurring jobs with natural language. "Check my server health every morning" becomes a scheduled cron task that runs and delivers results to you.

### 7. Multi-Platform Delivery

Hermes can reach you where you are — Telegram, Discord, Signal, or your local terminal. Results, alerts, and reports go where you need them.

### 8. Extensible & Open

Built on open standards (MCP, skills system), Hermes is easy to extend. Add new tools, connect external services, or write custom skills for your specific workflows.

## Who Is Hermes For?

- **Developers** who want an AI pair programmer that can actually execute code and manage repos.
- **DevOps engineers** who want to automate infrastructure tasks with natural language.
- **Researchers** who need to search, summarize, and organize information fast.
- **Power users** who want a single CLI agent that replaces a dozen tools.
